1. All judgements do not require examination, that is, investigation into the grounds of their truth.
2. But all judgement, nay, all comparisons require reflection, that is, a distinction of the faculty of cognition to which the given conceptions belong.
3. Identity and Difference.
4. Matter and Form.
5. Logicians formerly termed the universal, matter, the specific difference of this or that part of the universal, form.
